## 2. Lore & World: "The Fall"

### The Backstory

Decades ago, a catastrophic orbital warâ€”known as **"The Fall"**â€”shattered civilization. Massive fleets from unknown origins (or perhaps our own future?) clashed above the Earth. The debris rained down for months, leveling cities and poisoning the atmosphere. When the skies finally cleared, the survivors emerged from Vaults and bunkers to find a changed world: beautiful, overgrown, and littered with alien technology.

No one knows who won the war. No one knows if anyone is still up there. But the wrecks that fell... they hold secrets worth dying for.

### Crashed Ship Types (Scavenge Points)

The battlefield debris falls into two categories: **Terran (Human)** and **Unknown (Alien/Future Tech)**.

#### Terran Wrecks (Familiar, Repairable)

| Ship Type | Description | Loot / Interaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fighter Cockpit** | Single-seat attack craft. Often found in clusters after dogfights. | Pilot gear, emergency rations, small arms ammo. Sometimes a salvageable Fusion Cell. |
| **Bomber Fuselage** | Heavy attack craft. Larger, more dangerous to explore (unstable munitions). | Explosive components, heavy weapon parts, rare "Ordnance Cores." |
| **Corvette Wreck** | Small patrol ship. Half-buried in fields or smashed into buildings. | Scrap Metal, Basic Circuits, damaged weapons. Quick in-and-out. |
| **Battleship Section** | Massive. Only fragments survived re-entry. Forms multi-story structures. | Vehicle Bay access, Command Bridge loot (lore/data), heavy armor plating. |

| Ship Type | Description | Loot / Interaction |

### The Reconstruction System (Vehicle Rebuilding)

Players can find **Blueprint Fragments** inside larger wrecks. Collecting a full set allows them to **Reconstruct** a functional vehicle or mech in their Vault's Workshop.

| Reconstruction Project | Blueprint Fragments Needed | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Terran Scout Buggy** | 3 (Common) | A fast, lightly armored ground vehicle. Great for traversal. |

*Reconstruction requires significant token investment for parts and power, acting as a major token sink.*

### Scavengeable Upgrades (Examples)

* **Kinetic Shielding:** A personal energy shield that absorbs one hit. Requires a rare "Capacitor" from Frigate wrecks.
* **Gravity Boots:** Allows short boosts or wall-clings. Found in Carrier hangars.
* **Targeting Implant:** HUD overlay showing enemy weak points. Requires an "AI Core" (rare NFT drop).
* **Cloaking Field (Temporary):** 30 seconds of near-invisibility. Consumes "Phantom Cells," only found in Escape Pods.
* **Hardlight Tools:** Advanced construction/repair tools. Found in Orbital Station Debris.
* **Adaptive Camouflage (Unknown Tech):** Suit blends with environment. Found in Drone Swarm Hives.
* **Plasma Lance (Unknown Tech):** Devastating melee/short-range weapon. Requires Interceptor Shard components.

*Inspired by Alita: Battle Angel.* Players can enhance their own bodies with scavenged tech, blurring the line between human and machine.

| Frame Type | Source | Ability |

Individual body parts can be replaced or enhanced. Stacking augments creates unique builds.

| Augment Slot | Example Augment | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

Players can recover dormant AI personalities from wrecked ship computers. Once "awakened" in the Vault, these Ghosts provide passive bonuses and can be "slotted" into gear.

## 3. Visual Identity & Atmosphere

* **Theme:** **"Atompunk Apocalypse"** / **"Vault-Dweller Chic"**.
* **Inspirations:** *Fallout* (game series & TV show), *NieR: Automata*, *Horizon Zero Dawn*.
* **Visual Pillars:**
  * **Retro-Futurism:** 1950s-60s Americana meets advanced technology. Chunky CRT monitors, vacuum tubes, rotary dialsâ€”but controlling fusion reactors and androids.
  * **Overgrowth:** Nature reclaiming the old world. Rusted cars, cracked highways, trees bursting through diners and gas stations.
  * **The Vaults/Silos:** Underground bunkers are the "safe zones." Inside, the aesthetic is preservedâ€”clean(ish), humming generators, flickering lights. A stark contrast to the chaos outside.
  * **Atmosphere:** Heavy fog, volumetric god-rays, dust particles. The Wasteland feels vast and lonely. Vaults feel cramped but secure.
  * **UI Design:** **Diegetic & Retro.** The main HUD is a "Pip-Boy" style wrist device. Menus look like old terminal screens with green/amber text. Inventory is displayed as a physical backpack the player opens.
* **Quality Standard:** "Triple-I" (Indie Premium). The "retro" style allows us to use simpler geometry and flat textures intentionally, making the game run smoothly on web/mobile while looking stylized, not cheap. Post-Processing (Bloom, Film Grain, Color Grading) sells the cinematic look.

### The Vault System (Gameplay & Staking Integration)

* **Discovery:** Players find abandoned Vaults scattered across the Wasteland. Each Vault is a unique instance.
* **Activation:** To "claim" a Vault, the player must "Power it Up" by slotting (staking) a significant amount of ECHO-PRIME into the Vault Core.
* **Benefits:** An active Vault provides:
  * A safe respawn point.
  * Passive resource generation (Water, Power, Salvage).
  * Storage for gear that won't be lost on death.
  * A cosmetic "Home" to decorate and show off.
* **Risk:** If you fail to maintain the Vault (pay occasional upkeep fees), rival players can "Raid" it, potentially stealing a portion of your staked tokens.

## 3. The Dual-Token System

To protect the economy and allow for safe testing, we utilize two distinct tokens:

### ðŸª™ Token A: "ECHO-TEST" (The Beta Token)

* **Purpose:** For Beta Testing, balancing economy, and "Soft Launch" phases.
* **Value:** No real-world financial value strictly (or pegged to a very low stable value).
* **Utility:** Used to test bugs without risking the main liquidity pool.
* **Bridge:** Early adopters who earn "ECHO-TEST" will receive an airdrop of the Real Token based on their leaderboard position when we go live.

### ðŸ’Ž Token B: "ECHO-PRIME" (The Production Token)

* **Purpose:** The real, tradable cryptocurrency on Solana.
* **Obtainable:** Only by "Extracting" high-tier loot from the Zone or buying on exchanges.
* **Utility:** Purchasing Licenses, Insuring Gear, Trading Artifacts.

## 4. Economic Loop & Value Accrual

### The "Buy-In" (Revenue Generator)

* **Cost:** ~$15 USD (paid in SOL/USDC).
* **Action:** The system automatically swaps this $15 for ECHO-PRIME on the open market (Constant Buy Pressure).
* **Reward:** Player receives an **"Operator License" (NFT)** and a Starter Kit.

### The "Staking" (Retention Mechanism)

* **Naming:** We do *not* call it "Staking." We call it **"Garrisoning"** or **"Powering Up."**
* **Mechanic:**
  * To activate a **Personal Base**, players must "Slot" (Stake) 500 ECHO-PRIME into the Base Core.
  * While slotted, these tokens are removed from circulation (Supply Shock).
  * The Base produces specific resources (Ammo, Water) passively while powered.
  * **Un-staking:** If a player wants their tokens back, they must "Power Down" (a 3-7 day cooldown process), preventing panic selling.

### The Faucet (Earning)

* **Artifacts:** Players find rare "Data Drives" or "Tech Fragments" in the Zone.
* **Extraction:** They must physically carry them to an exit.
* **Liquidation:** Selling these artifacts to "The Syndicate" (Game Treasury) rewards them with ECHO-PRIME.
  * *Dynamic Pricing:* The Syndicate lowers its buying price if the Treasury is running low, automatically balancing inflation.

### The Sink (Burning)

* **Maintenance:** "Nano-Repair" for suits costs tokens.
* **Insurance:** "Clone Insurance" preserves gear on death.
* **Trading Tax:** 5% fee on the Player-to-Player marketplace. 2.5% goes to Devs, 2.5% is Burned forever.

## 5. Technical Infrastructure (Lean & Premium)

* **Game Engine:** **React Three Fiber (Web)**.
  * *Pros:* Zero install for users. Instant updates. Runs on same stack as our sites.
  * *Graphics:* Uses `postprocessing` library for that "expensive" look.
* **Backend:** **Convex** (Database & Realtime Functions).
  * *Pros:* Handles thousands of concurrent item updates cheaply. Serverless.
  * *Logic:* Verifies all "Extractions" to prevent cheating.
* **Hosting:** Existing **VPS**. Node.js Game Server instances.
* **Blockchain:** **Solana**.
  * *Integration:* Wallet only needed for Login and Deposits/Withdrawals. Gameplay is "Gasless" (managed by our server key, batched).

// convex/syndicate.ts
export const getSyndicatePrice = query({
  args: { itemType: v.string() },
  handler: async (ctx, args) => {
    const treasury = await ctx.db.query("treasury").first();
    const basePrice = ITEM_BASE_PRICES[args.itemType];
    
    // If treasury is low, reduce buy price
    // If treasury is high, increase buy price
